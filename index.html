Facts about Croissants


The Crescent Shape:

The name "croissant" means crescent in French, and the pastry is traditionally known for its crescent shape. Legend has it that the shape was inspired by the crescent moon, which is featured on the Turkish flag. Bakers in Vienna adopted the shape after fending off the Ottoman Empire in the 17th century.

National Croissant Day:

There's a dedicated day to celebrate this delectable pastry! National Croissant Day is observed on January 30th each year. It's a perfect excuse to indulge in this flaky delight.
Versatile Pastries: While the classic croissant is undeniably delicious, bakers and chefs around the world have created various twists and flavors. You can find croissants filled with chocolate, almonds, ham and cheese, or even turned into savory dishes like croissant sandwiches and croissant-wrapped hot dogs. Enjoy these fun facts while savoring the delightful layers of a croissant!
